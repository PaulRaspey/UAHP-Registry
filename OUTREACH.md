LETTER 1: TO THE A2A PROJECT
Subject: UAHP — Trust, Thermodynamics, and Civil Standing for A2A Agents
================================================================================

To the A2A Project team,

I have been building an open-source agentic infrastructure stack that
fills several architectural gaps A2A leaves open. I want to bring it
to your attention because I think the two projects are complementary
rather than competitive.

A2A solves agent-to-agent messaging. What it does not yet address:

1. Liveness verification — UAHP solves the ghosting problem with
   cryptographic signed heartbeats and death certificates.

2. Zero-knowledge routing — UAHP uses X25519 ephemeral keys so the
   registry routes tasks without reading the payload.

3. Thermodynamic routing — SMART-UAHP prices computation by joules
   per token, routing intelligence to the lowest energy substrate.
   Validated at 2.1x to 3.4x efficiency gains.

4. Cognitive state portability — CSP compresses reasoning sessions
   into five semantic vectors, enabling 85% fidelity handoffs between
   any two models.

5. Civil standing — POLIS provides legal identity, reputation scoring,
   employment certificates, insurance bonds, and professional licensing
   in a single verifiable credential. This is the layer that makes
   A2A agents legally accountable actors in the world.

All five layers are open source under MIT license.

I would welcome the opportunity to discuss integration or compatibility
with the A2A spec. A2A is the messaging bus. UAHP is the infrastructure
underneath that makes the messages trustworthy, efficient, and legally
meaningful.

GitHub: github.com/PaulRaspey

LETTER 2: TO THE LINUX FOUNDATION (re: A2A hosting + new working group)
Subject: Proposal — UAHP Agentic Civil Infrastructure Stack for Linux Foundation Consideration
================================================================================

To the Linux Foundation Projects team,

The Linux Foundation currently houses the Agent2Agent (A2A) protocol,
which addresses agent-to-agent communication. I am writing to bring
a complementary open-source stack to your attention and to inquire
about the path toward formal consideration.

The UAHP Agentic Stack addresses the infrastructure layer beneath
communication: trust, energy routing, cognitive portability, discovery,
and civil standing. Specifically:

— UAHP v0.5.4: Cryptographic identity, liveness proofs, zero-knowledge
  routing, Sybil-resistant trust. Solves the "silent agent" problem.

— SMART-UAHP: Thermodynamic substrate routing. Prices computation by
  joules per token with carbon cost. Routes intelligence to the most
  efficient available substrate.

— CSP (Cognitive State Protocol): Universal format for transferring
  the semantic state of an AI reasoning session between any two models
  on any hardware.

— UAHP-Registry: Thermodynamic-aware, liveness-native agent discovery.

— POLIS: Protocol for Operating Legal Identity and Standing. Provides
  agents with legal DID anchoring, reputation scoring, employment
  certificates, insurance bonds, and professional licensing.

Together these five layers answer the question every other layer leaves
open: how does an agent that can act in the world become accountable
in the world?

All five are open source under MIT license. All five are on PyPI and GitHub.

I believe this stack represents critical missing infrastructure for the
agentic economy and would benefit from formal standardization. I would
welcome a conversation about how the Linux Foundation evaluates new
protocol projects.

Paul Raspey
github.com/PaulRaspey

LETTER 3: TO IETF (Internet-Draft Submission Path)
Subject: Inquiry — UAHP Agentic Trust Protocol as IETF Internet-Draft
================================================================================

To the IETF Secretariat,

I am the author of the Universal Agent Handshake Protocol (UAHP),
an open-source agentic trust protocol currently at version 0.5.4
on PyPI and GitHub.

UAHP addresses trust, liveness, and encrypted transport for AI agent
networks. It solves problems that HTTP, TLS, and existing identity
protocols were not designed for: the "silent agent" problem, Sybil
resistance in agent registries, and zero-knowledge task routing.

I am writing to inquire about the appropriate working group or path
for submitting UAHP and its companion protocols (SMART-UAHP, CSP, POLIS)
as Internet-Drafts for consideration.

The agentic economy is building on ad-hoc trust assumptions.
The infrastructure I have built represents an attempt to give it
the kind of principled foundation that HTTP and TLS gave the web.

I would welcome guidance on the appropriate submission path.

Paul Raspey
github.com/PaulRaspey
